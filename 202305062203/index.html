Alternating vs Direct Current

Alternating current (AC) is a form of electricity wherein the direction of flow of the current in the wire alternates many times per second. Alternating Current (AC). Direct current (DC), on the other hand, does not change its flow direction. Direct Current (DC)

Relationship

AC and DC currents can be converted into one another using an analog-to-digital converter.
Analog to Digital Converter (ADC)
