I2C Communication

Overview

I2C is a half-duplex Serial Communication protocol that involves two wires:

  1. SDA (data)
  2. SCL (clock)

Each device has an SDA and SCL wire, and all devices are connected to the same SDA and SCL buses. Each line is passively pulled up to HIGH to avoid Floating pins.

The SDA and SCL wires work together to define the start and stop conditions, and the bits of data in between.
