I2C Bus Arbitration

Bus arbitration is a feature of I2C communcation that safeguards the I2C bus lines from having collisions in the case of multiple masters initiating a transaction at the same time.

The master that writes a 0 first while the other is writing a 1 will win the arbitration and continue its message while the other master will stop and wait. This is because the master that puts a 1 is actually releasing the SDA line. See I2C Open-Drain for more information.
