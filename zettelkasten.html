What is a Zettelkasten?

Zettelkasten is German for "slip box". It's a note-taking system designed and used by the German sociologist Niklas Luhmann. He needed a system to help him manage vast amounts of information and be a source for generating innovative ideas.

The problem with traditional note-taking is that it leads to fragmented and cluttered information. You fragment information when you box in ideas (folders) and thereby isolate them. You clutter information when you have more folders than you can handle and don't know where to put what. The reality is that concepts overlap and intertwine and can't be categorized in just one way. Luhmann believed that to truly understand complex subjects, you need a system that encouraged an interplay of ideas.

In essence, a Zettelkasten is a living and breathing network of notes; a web of knowledge; a second brain. Each note can be linked to others, allowing for the emergence of unexpected connections, patterns, and insights, making it an invaluable tool for research, writing, and personal growth. Ideally, each note is atomic. The concept of atomicity in this context means there is one and only one idea being expressed per note. This makes each note complete in and of itself, thereby grounding it. Such notes can now serve as the foundation for other, more complex ideas. This system of note-taking grows organically without maintenance because...

  1. Each note can stand alone with the opportunity to be linked.
  2. Those that are linked infrequently are lost in the Zettelkasten and are forgotten.
  3. Those that are linked often will continue to come up and are the ones worth remembering.
